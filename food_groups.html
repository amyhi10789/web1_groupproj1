WHAT ARE FOOD GROUPS?

Food groups are categories used to organize different types of foods based on their main nutrients and the roles they play in keeping your body healthy. The idea is that eating a balanced variety from all the groups helps you get the right mix of nutrientsâ€”like carbohydrates, proteins, fats, vitamins, and minerals.

Fruits

  • Examples: Apples, bananas, berries, oranges.
  • Main nutrients: Vitamins (especially vitamin C), fibers, natural sugars, and antioxidants.
  • Why they're important: Help boost your immune system and digestion.

Vegetables

  • Examples: Carrots, spinach, broccoli, peppers.
  • Main nutrients: Vitamins A and C, fiber, potassium, and folate.
  • Why they're important: Support healthy skin, eyes, and organs, and reduce disease risk..

Grains

  • Examples: Bread, rice pasta, oats, cereal.
  • Main nutrients: Carbohydrates (for energy), fiber, B vitamins, and iron.
  • Why they're important: Provide long lasting energy and help with digestion (especially whole grains).

Protein

  • Examples: Meat, poultry, fish, eggs, beans, nuts, tofu.
  • Main nutrients: Proteins, iron, zinc, and some B vitamins.
  • Why they're important: Build and repair body tissues like muscles and cells.

Dairy

  • Examples: Milk, yogurt, cheese, fortified soy milk.
  • Main nutrients: Calcium, vitamin D, protein, and potassium.
  • Why they're important: Help build strong bones and teeth.
